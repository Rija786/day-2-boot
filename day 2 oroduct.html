football


1. Number of Players Each team has 11 players (including the goalkeeper). A match cannot continue if a team has fewer than 7 players. 2. Duration of the Match Two halves of 45 minutes each. 15-minute halftime break. Added time (injury time) is given at the end of each half based on stoppages. 3. Ball In and Out of Play The ball is out of play when it fully crosses the goal line or touchline. It's in play at all other times, including when it rebounds off the goalpost or referee. 4. Scoring a Goal A goal is scored when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the posts and under the crossbar. 5. Offside Rule A player is offside if: They are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender. They are actively involved in play (passing, interfering, etc.). No offside if: They are in their own half. They receive the ball from a goal kick, throw-in, or corner. 6. Fouls and Misconduct Common fouls: Tripping, pushing, handball, or holding an opponent. Serious fouls lead to yellow (warning) or red cards (sending off). 7. Free Kicks and Penalties Direct Free Kick: Can be shot directly into the goal. Indirect Free Kick: Must touch another player before a goal can be scored. Penalty Kick: Awarded for fouls inside the penalty area. One-on-one with the goalkeeper from the penalty spot. 8. Throw-Ins Awarded when the ball crosses the touchline. Must be thrown with both hands from behind and over the head, with both feet on the ground. 9. Corner Kicks and Goal Kicks Corner Kick: Awarded when the ball crosses the goal line off the defending team. Goal Kick: Awarded when the ball crosses the goal line off the attacking team. 10. Substitutions Typically 5 substitutions are allowed per team (can vary depending on tournament rules). Substitutions happen during stoppages with referee permission.
